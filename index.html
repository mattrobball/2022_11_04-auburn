Generation in prime characteristic

Given an algebraic structure, one of the first steps to understanding it is to find generators.

For a group $G$, we look for a set $ \lbrace g_a \mid a \in A \rbrace $ of elements such that for any $ x \in G $ we have \[ x = g_{a_1}^{n_1} \cdots g_{a_l}^{n_l} \] for some $ n_i \in \mathbb{Z}$.

For a module $ M $ over a ring $ R $, we seek a surjection \[ R^{\oplus A} \to M \]

For a triangulated category $ T $, our generative operation is forming cones \[ X \overset{f}{\to} Y \to C(f) \to X[1] \]

Definition . We say that a (full) subcategory $ S $ generates $ T $ if the smallest subcategory of $ T $

  • closed under finite sums, shifts, and summands
  • closed under triangles and
  • containing $ S $

is $ T $ itself.

We write $ \langle S \rangle $ for this smallest subcategory in general and say \[ \langle S \rangle = T \] if $ S $ generates.

We have $ \langle R \rangle = \operatorname{perf} R $, the subcategory of bounded complexes with finitely-generated projective components.

If we are Noetherian (and commutative), $ R $ generates $ D^b(\operatorname{mod} R) $ if and only if $ R $ is regular by Auslander-Buchsbaum.

Challenge: I hand you $ R $. You hand back a generator for $ D^b(\operatorname{mod} R) $.

Some examples to meditate upon:

  • $ k[x]/(x^2) $
  • $ k[x,y,z]/(xy,xz) $

Assume that $ R $ lives over a field $ k $ with $\operatorname{char} k > 0 $.

Corollary: If $ R $ is Noetherian and $ F_\ast R $ is finitely-generated, then for $ e \gg 0 $, $F_\ast^e R$ generates $ D^b(\operatorname{mod} R )$.

This doesn't work globally.
If $ C $ is smooth and proper with $ g(C) > 0 $, then $F_\ast^e \mathcal O_C $ does not generate for any $ e$.

Two natural questions:

  1. For what $ X $ does $ F_\ast^e \mathcal O_X $ generate?
  2. Does $ F_\ast^e \operatorname{perf} X $ generate?

Regarding Question 1, we have a guess.

Conjecture: For $ X $ smooth and projective, $F_\ast^e \mathcal O_X $ generates if and only if $ D^b(\operatorname{coh} X) $ possesses a full exceptional collection.

Question 2 has an answer.

Theorem: (MRB-Lank) For $ e \gg 0 $, $ F_\ast^e \operatorname{perf} X $ generates $D^b(\operatorname{coh} X) $.

This has the marvelous by-product that $ F_\ast^e $ converts weak generators into strong ones.
If $ X $ is not regular, then any perfect generator $ G $ takes an unbounded number of cones to generate all of $ \operatorname{perf} X $.
However, $ F_\ast^e G $ will only need a uniformly bounded number of cones to generate $ D^b(\operatorname{coh} X )$.

Corollary: If $ \mathcal L $ is a very ample line bundle on $ X $, then \[ \bigoplus_{i=0}^{\dim X} F_\ast^e \mathcal L^i \] is a strong generator for $ D^b(\operatorname{coh} X )$.

Relations to other works:

  • One can find generators of $D^b(\operatorname{coh} X) $ pretty generally: Rouquier, Iyengar-Takahashi, Neeman,... but not as constructively.
  • Independently Iyengar-Pollitz-Mukhopadhyay recently announced the affine case. They also say that you can take $e =1 $ for complete intersections.

A GUT for flops and derived categories

Recall that a flip relative to a divisor $ D $ is a diagram \[ \begin{CD} @. X \\ @. @VV f V \\ X^+ @>> f^+ > Y \end{CD} \] inducing an isomorphisms in codimension $\geq 1$ and such that $-D$ is $f$-ample and $D$ is $f^+$-ample.

A flip is a flop if $f^\ast K_X = (f^+)^\ast K_{X^+}$.

Geometric Invariant Theory provides a natural source of flips. For example, given a $\mathbb{Z}$-graded ring $R$ we get a flip (under some mild assumptions) where \[ X = \operatorname{Proj}~ \bigoplus_{n \leq 0} R_n \\ X^+ = \operatorname{Proj}~ \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} R_n \\ Y = \operatorname{Spec} R_0 \]

Groups with higher rank often provide more intricate examples. For example, let $k$ be a field and $V,W$ be finite-dimensional vector spaces over $k$ and assume that $\dim V < \dim W$.

Set $ G = \operatorname{Aut}(V) $. For $A \in \operatorname{Hom}(W,V)$ and $B \in \operatorname{Hom}(V,W)$ set \[ Z = \lbrace (A,B) \mid AB = 0 \rbrace \] We get two GIT quotients $Z^{ss}(+)/G$ and $Z^{ss}(-)/G$ related by a flop.

For $2 \leq \dim V \leq \dim W -2 $, the two GIT quotients of $Z$ give a stratified Mukai flop.

Conjecture: (Bondal-Orlov) Two smooth projective varieties related by a flop are derived equivalent.

This was amplified by Kawamata to a pair of K-equivalent varieties.

For a brief moment in time, it was hoped that the structure of sheaf of the fiber product \[ \mathcal O_{X \times_Y X^+} \] furnished a Fourier-Mukai kernel.

Kawamata/Namikawa: For stratified Mukai flops with $\dim V=2$, it is not. 💔

However, Cautis-Kamnitzer-Licata produced Fourier-Mukai kernels for stratified Mukai flops.

MRB-Diemer-Favero described a procedure to associate an integral kernel to any affine GIT problem given a linear algebraic monoid $M$ with $M^\times = G $.

If $G$ is acting on $Z$ via $\sigma : G \times Z \to Z $, then let \[ Q = \operatorname{im}\left(k[Z \times Z \times M] \overset{\phi}{\to} k[Z \times G]\right) \] where \[ r \otimes r^\prime \otimes f \overset{\phi}{\mapsto} r \sigma^\ast(r') f \]

By restricting $Q$ to $Z^{ss}(+) \times Z^{ss}(-)$, we get a integral kernel \[ P \in D(\operatorname{Qcoh} Z^{ss}(+)/G \times Z^{ss}(-)/G) \]

Question: When is $\Phi_P$ fully-faithful?

For example, if $\dim V = 1$ and $\dim W = n+1$, then we get the graded ring \[ R = \frac{k[x_0,\ldots,x_n,y_0,\ldots,y_n]}{(x_0y_0+\cdots+x_ny_n)} \] the associate flop is called a Mukai flop and $P$ is graph of the induced birational map -- which is not a FM kernel.

Fix: one needs to derive $R$

Then we get $\mathcal O_{X \times_Y X^+}$ which is FM here.

For general stratified Mukai flop, $R = k[A,B]/(AB) $, we couldn't get our hands on the representation theory of $Q_{der}$ so we looked at its relative Koszul dual.

\[ S = k[A,B,T] \] for $T \in \operatorname{End}(V)$.

An explicit presentation for $Q$ is

\[ \scriptsize Q = \frac{k[A_L,B_L,T_L,A_R,B_R,T_R,C]} {(CA_L-A_R,B_L-B_RC,CT_L-T_RC,c_i(T_L)-c_i(T_R))} \]

where $ C \in \operatorname{End}(V) $ and $c_i$ are the coefficients of the characteristic polynomial.

Theorem:(MRB-Chidambaram-Favero)
For $\dim V = 2$, $P$ is FM for the flop associated to $S$.

Corollary: The Koszul dual to $P$ is an FM kernel for the stratified Mukai flop.

We know that $\mathcal H^0P^!$ is a CKL kernel.
